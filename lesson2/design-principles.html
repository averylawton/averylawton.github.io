Design Principles in Websites

White Space and Clean Design

Tinker

Tinker home page

In the website Tinker, they use whitespace to clearly separate items, add visual interest to icons, and create a professional layout for any device size. The most notable use of whitespace is the space in between information and pictures. It makes for a long scroll time, but the sparciety of information between each scroll makes the reader take in everything on the page. It is also worthy to note that the images on the page are often cutouts, not square pictures. Doing this, there is more whitespace to bring consistency to the overall design.

PARC: Proximity

Stereo

Stereo home page

Stereo, a French website, uses proximity skillfully and consistently. Throughout the home page design, the information is organized in beautifully designed boxes. Each of the boxes has a headline, a brief sentence of information, a picture, and a link leading to a page where you can learn more.
